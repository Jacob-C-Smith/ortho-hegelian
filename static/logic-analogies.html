Logic Analogies

Hegel’s Objective Logic as a tree:

  1. Quality - a leaf
  2. Quantity - the number of leaves
  3. Measure - the proportion of leaves to branches, to trunk, to seed, etc.
  4. Essence - the seed
  5. Appearance - the emergence of the tree from the seed
  6. Actuality - the genesis of the fruit on the branch, i.e. the return of the seed in the tree ## Hegel’s Objective Logic as a programming language:
  7. Quality - variables, strings, data types (pure content)
  8. Quantity - numbers, decimals, numerical operators
  9. Measure - arrays, lists, sets
  10. Essence - functions (pure form)
  11. Appearance - objects, loops, control flow
  12. Actuality - classes, higher order functions, etc. ## Hegel’s Objective Logic as formal logic:
  13. Quality - boolean and conjunction (†, ¬, ∧, ∨)
  14. Quantity - arithmetic (+, -, ⋅, ÷)
  15. Measure - set theory and proportion (∪, ∩, :)
  16. Essence - identity and difference (=, ≠)
  17. Appearance - conditional (→), biconditional (↔︎)
  18. Actuality - possibility (◇) and necessity (□)
